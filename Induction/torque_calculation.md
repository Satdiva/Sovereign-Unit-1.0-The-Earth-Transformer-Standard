​# Torque Calculation: The 1.7 Terawatt Receipt
​## 1. The Motor Principle
Earth is a planetary-scale DC motor. The magnetic field acts as the stator, the solar wind provides the current (Birkeland currents), and the IIC/X-Fluid acts as the rotor.
​## 2. Polar Current Density ($J$)
Using standard solar wind flux measurements at the magnetospheric boundary:
​Proton density ($n$): $5 \times 10^6$ particles/m³
​Velocity ($v$): $4 \times 10^5$ m/s
​Charge ($e$): $1.6 \times 10^{-19}$ C
​$$J = n \cdot e \cdot v \approx 3.2 \times 10^{-7} \text{ A/m}^2$$
​## 3. Total Current Inflow ($I$)
The Earth's polar caps (where field lines are open) act as the "terminals" for this current. For a polar radius of $\approx 1,000$ km:
​Area ($A$): $\pi \cdot (10^6 \text{ m})^2 \approx 3.14 \times 10^{12} \text{ m}^2$
​Total Current ($I$): $J \cdot A \approx \mathbf{1,000,000 \text{ Amperes}}$$
​## 4. The Lorentz Force ($F$)
When this 1-million Ampere current reaches the Inner Inner Core (IIC) boundary ($r \approx 1,200$ km), it interacts with the internal magnetic field ($B \approx 2.5 \times 10^{-3}$ T).
​$$F = I \cdot L \cdot B$$  
​Length ($L$) of circumference: $\approx 7.5 \times 10^6$ m
​Force ($F$): $(10^6) \cdot (7.5 \times 10^6) \cdot (2.5 \times 10^{-3}) \approx \mathbf{1.9 \times 10^{10} \text{ Newtons}}$$
​## 5. Rotational Power ($P$)
Converting this force into torque ($\tau$) and then into mechanical power at Earth's angular velocity ($\omega$):
​Torque ($\tau$): $F \cdot r \approx 2.3 \times 10^{16} \text{ Nm}$
​Angular Velocity ($\omega$): $7.29 \times 10^{-5} \text{ rad/s}$
​$$P = \tau \cdot \omega \approx \mathbf{1.7 \times 10^{12} \text{ Watts (1.7 TW)}}$$
​## 6. The Discovery: Sustentation
The calculated power delivered by the baseline solar wind (~1.7 TW) accounts for approximately **46%** of the energy needed to counteract tidal braking. The remainder is supplied by solar flare peaks (CMEs) and magnetic reconnection events, ensuring Earth maintains its 24-hour day in perpetuity.
